## Project Overview: Pro App Database Architecture & Analytics
**Role:** Database Designer & Data Analyst[cite: 1]
**Core Technologies:** SQL, Relational Database Design, Entity-Relationship (ER) Modeling[cite: 1]

This project involved designing, implementing, and analyzing a robust relational database for "Pro App," a complex sharing economy platform[cite: 1]. The platform facilitates dynamic interactions between customers posting tasks, tradespeople bidding on labor/contracts, and suppliers selling materials[cite: 1].

---

## Phase 1: Database Architecture & Implementation
The foundational phase focused on structuring the data ecosystem to ensure efficiency, data integrity, and seamless user onboarding[cite: 1]. 

*   **ER Modeling:** Designed a comprehensive Entity-Relationship model using Chen's notation to map complex, multi-layered interactions[cite: 1]. 
*   **Schema Design:** Engineered 13 distinct SQL tables to categorize users (Customers, Trade People, Suppliers) and track platform activities (Tasks, Bidding, Memberships, Training Courses)[cite: 1].
*   **Data Integrity:** Implemented strict Primary Keys (PK) and Foreign Keys (FK) to prevent orphaned records and automate transaction tracking across the platform[cite: 1].
*   **Data Simulation:** Populated the database with extensive dummy data to simulate real-world transactions, bidding wars, and user activities[cite: 1].

---

## Phase 2: Business Insights & Query Optimization
Using advanced SQL queries, the raw database was analyzed to extract actionable business intelligence regarding user behavior and platform profitability[cite: 1]. 

*   **Transaction Analytics:** Discovered that 45.24% of platform transactions were direct customer-to-supplier material purchases, outweighing labor-hiring transactions[cite: 1].
*   **Purchasing Behavior:** Identified that while customers placed a higher volume of material orders (38 orders), tradespeople purchased materials in much larger bulk quantities per order[cite: 1].
*   **Cross-Selling Gaps:** Query results revealed a 0% crossover rate between customers hiring labor and customers purchasing materials, highlighting a massive opportunity for bundled services[cite: 1].
*   **Revenue Dependency:** Calculated that membership fees accounted for 84.6% of the platform's total revenue, with task-based commissions making up the remaining 15.4%[cite: 1].
*   **Service Satisfaction:** Determined that material-purchasing tasks received the highest average customer rating (3.38/5), while labor-only tasks received the lowest (2.90/5)[cite: 1].
*   **Market Leaders:** Extracted the top 3 suppliers winning the most bids and the top 3 highest-earning tradespeople on the platform[cite: 1].

---

## Strategic Business Recommendations
Based on the SQL data analysis, three primary business strategies were formulated[cite: 1]:
1.  **Incentivize Dual-Service Usage:** Introduce discounts or bundled packages to encourage customers to purchase materials alongside hiring labor[cite: 1].
2.  **Enhance Labor Satisfaction:** Implement stricter vetting processes and training programs for tradespeople to improve the low customer satisfaction scores tied to labor tasks[cite: 1].
3.  **Diversify Revenue Streams:** Increase task-related commissions or introduce premium add-on services to reduce the platform's heavy financial reliance on membership fees[cite: 1].
